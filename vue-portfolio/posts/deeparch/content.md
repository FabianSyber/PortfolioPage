### Project Description

**Deep Architecture** is a master thesis project exploring machine learning and neural networks in an architectural framework. The research is divided into theory and design studies, investigating how computational techniques can be integrated into architectural workflows.

### Key Features

- Application of neural networks in architectural design
- Machine learning-driven spatial organization
- Digital, site-independent experimentation
- Open-source codebase for further development

### Process

The project developed a **pipeline-based workflow** that applies machine learning at different architectural stages. The four main steps include:

1. **Footprint Division** – Breaking down the building mass into smaller components.
2. **Spatial Organization** – Using neural networks to generate functional floor plans.
3. **Facade Generation** – Training networks on facade logic to create optimized designs.
4. **3D Model & Rendering** – Linking floor plans and facades to extrude models and applying AI-driven rendering techniques.

### Implementation

- **Digital-Only Workflow**: The project is purely computational, allowing for flexible experimentation.
- **Neural Networks**: AI-based tools generate and refine architectural components.
- **Open Source Contribution**: The full project is available on [GitHub](https://github.com/FabianSyber/DeepArchitecture) for students and practitioners.

### Design Principles

- **Computational Design**: Utilizing AI to optimize and generate architectural solutions.
- **Process Over Outcome**: Focusing on the methodology rather than a final built project.
- **Automation & Parametric Thinking**: Streamlining architectural workflows through digital tools.
