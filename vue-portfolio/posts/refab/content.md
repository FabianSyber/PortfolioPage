### Project Description

**ReFAB** was a collaborative initiative focused on circular design and resource efficiency in the construction industry. Supported by Vinnova (2021–2025), the project aimed to establish sustainable processes for recycled glass wool while addressing construction waste challenges. A key aspect of the project was the integration of digitalization and computational design to enhance efficiency and scalability.

Through parametric workflows and file-to-factory methodologies, ReFAB leveraged automation in design and fabrication, enabling rapid prototyping and optimized material usage. Computational design techniques facilitated precise control over acoustic performance, while digital fabrication minimized waste and streamlined production. The project also explored data-sharing platforms to improve material traceability and transparency across the value chain.

### Key Features

- Development of circular acoustic materials
- Mobile manufacturing demonstrations
- Parametric design integration
- Digitalization and file-to-factory workflows
- Material traceability and data-sharing
- Fire classification testing
- Cross-industry collaboration

### Process

The project advanced from TRL 4 to TRL 6, demonstrating the feasibility of sustainable acoustic materials. Parametric design played a key role in optimizing material efficiency and customization. A data-sharing platform (IoL) was tested for material traceability, and fire classification tests assessed compliance. Collaboration across the value chain was crucial for identifying challenges and opportunities.

### Implementation

ReFAB explored computational design for acoustic optimization through:

- Point-Based Acoustics: Precision-driven method for iterative material refinement.
- Wave Function Collapse (WFC): Modular, rule-based design for faster iteration and fabrication.

### Design Principles

The project prioritized function-driven design, circularity, and digital fabrication, ensuring:

- Design for disassembly and reuse
- Minimal waste through file-to-factory workflows
- Sustainable material selection and lifecycle considerations

### Outcome & Future Directions

ReFAB demonstrated scalable solutions for circular acoustic materials, validating mobile manufacturing and material tracking. While commercial viability requires further development, the project laid the foundation for integrating sustainable materials in construction, emphasizing collaboration and innovation for future adoption.
