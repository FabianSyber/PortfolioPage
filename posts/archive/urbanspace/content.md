### Project Description

**Urban Space Design** is a project investigating how cultural and transportation hotspots can be diffused to create new formal and informal gathering points in Lindholmen, Gothenburg. This urban planning and public space project is driven by data-driven studies to understand and enhance urban interactions.

### Key Features

- Data-driven urban mapping
- Creation of cultural diffusion networks
- Integration of parametric urban analysis
- Suspension-based modular urban framework

### Process

The project analyzed cultural and transportation activity in Lindholmen using various mapping techniques. By overlaying time-based construction clusters, Google Maps activity reports, and traditional topographical data, a new **cultural topography** was generated to identify potential new gathering points.

### Implementation

- **Cultural Mapping**: Identifying active hotspots and tracking their diffusion
- **Organic Growth Networks**: Using fungal growth simulation scripts to guide new hubs
- **Parametric Structure**: A modular suspension-based system for adaptable public spaces

### Design Principles

- **Layered Urban Analysis**: Merging infrastructure, activity, and topography data
- **User-Driven Adaptability**: Spaces shaped by inhabitants through modular interventions
- **Public Space Enhancement**: Strengthening urban fabric by diffusing cultural and transport hubs
